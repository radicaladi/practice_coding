Bruce Lee

"Be formless... shapeless, like water.

Bruce Lee was a Hong Kong martial artist and instructor, an actor, director, and philosopher. The founder of Jeet Kune Do, a martial arts philosophy that sought to use no way as way and adapt what is useful while casting off what is useless. Lee is considered by commentators, critics, media, and other martial artists to be the most influential martial artist of all time and a pop culture icon of the 20th century, who bridged the gap between East and West. Often credited with helping to change the way Asians were presented in American films. Time (magazine) also named Lee one of the 100 most important people of the 20th century.
(1940 - 1973)

10 things you didn't know about Bruce Lee:

  • Bruce Lee was born Lee Jun-Fan in San Francisco to Cantonese opera performer Lee Hoi-Chuen and Grace Ho.
  • His first acting role came at the age of 1 in the Chinese movie, Golden Gate Girl.
  • His parents made him live with family friends in Seattle, WA for getting into too many fights in Hong Kong.
  • Bruce earned a diploma from Edison Technical School in Seattle before gaining admission to the University of Washington Seattle.
  • At the University of Washington, he studied drama and philosophy but eventually dropped out of school.
  • Bruce met Linda Emery at the University of Washington and the couple married the same year, eventually having two children, Brandon and Shannon Lee.
  • His inconclusive combat match with Wong Jack Man inspired Bruce's development of the Jeet Kune Do philosophy, introducing it to the world in 1967.
  • Bruce was an ROTC member at the U-of-Washington and was drafted in 1963 by the U.S. Army but despite his desire to serve, would be denied for failing his pre-induction physical for a non-descended testicle, poor eyesight, and sinus disorder.
  • Bruce starred, wrote, directed, and choreographed the movie Way of the Dragon, which Chuck Norris played his rival; it was the first Hong Kong film to be shot in Europe.
  • Bruce passed away at the age of 32 in 1973 by allergic reaction to painkillers. Acting in a total of 13 movies with multiple scripts unfinished. He was laid to rest in Lakeview Cemetery, Seattle.
