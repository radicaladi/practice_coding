The Notorious

Dublin, Ireland

Conor Anthony McGregor was born in Dublin on 14 July 1988 the son of Tony and Margaret McGregor. He was raised in Crumlin and attended Irish-language schools — the Gaelscoil Scoil Mológa primary school and Gaelcholáiste Coláiste de hÍde in Tallaght secondary school. In his youth, he played football for Lourdes Celtic Football Club and at the age of 12 began boxing at Crumlin Boxing Gym as a way to defend himself against bullies and improve his self-confidence. After moving to Lucan, Dublin in 2006, he commenced a plumbing apprenticeship after attending Gaelcholáiste Coláiste Cois Life. Where his journey into mixed martial arts would begin.

Conor McGregor

A Journey Begins

After one fight as an amateur, Mcgregor turned professional at the age of 20 in 2008, signed by the Irish Cage of Truth promotion. The same year, McGregor began training at the Straight Blast Gym in Dublin under trainer, John Kavanagh. By the year 2012, McGregor would amass an impressive 10-2 record with all results ending by KO, TKO, or Submission. With an exciting style, McGregor earned his first title show for the organization Cage Warriors mid-year where he won the Cage Warriors Featherweight championship by submission at the end of the second round. On the last day of the same year, McGregor would make history with the organization by winning the Cage Warriors Lightweight championship by KO in the first round. Becoming only the second multi-divisional champion of Cage Warriors and the only fighter in Cage Warriors history to simultaneous hold two division titles, being the Featherweight and Lightweight champion.
